# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers try to trick people into sharing sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. A well-known example is the 2016 Google Docs phishing scam, where users received emails that looked like official messages from Google.

In most cases, the attacker starts by sending a fake email or message that appears genuine. It may create urgency, such as warning the user about a security issue or asking them to verify their account. When the user clicks on the provided link, they are taken to a fake website that looks almost identical to the real one. Without realizing the difference, the user enters their login details, which are then captured by the attacker. These stolen credentials can be used to access accounts, steal data, or even commit financial fraud.

Phishing attacks often target students, working professionals, and anyone who frequently uses email or online services. People who are not aware of online safety practices are more likely to fall victim.

The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. This highlights the importance of being cautious online, checking links carefully, and using security measures like two-factor authentication to stay protected.
