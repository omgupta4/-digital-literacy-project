# Case Study: Phishing Attack

- One of the most common cybercrimes is phishing. In phishing, the attacker attempts to deceive the victim into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank information, or personal information. An example of phishing is the 2016 Google Docs phishing scam. In this scam, users of Google received emails from the company. 

- The attacker in most cases of phishing starts by sending a fake email or message. The attacker may claim that there is a security threat on the victim’s account or that the victim should verify his or her account. Once the victim clicks on the link provided in the email, he or she is redirected to a fake website. The fake website looks almost identical to the original website. The victim does not know the difference between the original website and the fake one. The victim then proceeds to enter his or her login information. The information is then captured by the attacker. The attacker can then use the information to gain access to the victim’s account. The attacker can also use the information for financial fraud. 

- Phishing attacks can happen to students, working professionals, or anyone who frequently uses email. People who are not aware of internet safety are more likely to fall victims. 

- The consequences of phishing can cause serious problems such as financial fraud, identity theft, or unauthorized access to personal information.
